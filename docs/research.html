Research guardrails

This repository uses dynamical-systems-inspired methods to study recurrence, clustering, and joint stress in observed economic time series. That makes the methods useful, but it also creates clear limits on what the outputs justify.

Supported claims

  • one series exhibits more clustered extremes than another
  • return times to stress regions differ across systems or regimes
  • joint stress events involve simultaneous threshold exceedances
  • rare-event diagnostics and baseline econometric summaries capture different patterns

Unsupported claims

  • observed economies are literally deterministic maps
  • recurrence diagnostics identify causal mechanisms
  • extremal-index estimates isolate a policy effect
  • return-time summaries forecast the exact next crisis date

How to frame results

Use language such as describes, is consistent with, highlights clustering, or suggests recurrence structure. Avoid language such as proves, shows causality, or predicts the next crisis date.

Before publishing a claim

  • Confirm the claim is traceable to a saved figure, table, or CSV.
  • Check whether the result is based on real empirical data or a synthetic fallback.
  • Inspect threshold sensitivity before writing strong prose.
  • Keep descriptive diagnostics separate from causal interpretation.
