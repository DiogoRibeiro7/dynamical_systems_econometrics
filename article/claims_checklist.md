# Article Claims Checklist

Use this checklist before turning repository outputs into article prose.

## Safe framing

- describe clustering, recurrence, dependence, or joint stress
- describe comparisons with standard econometric baselines
- describe threshold sensitivity and sample limitations

## Unsafe framing

- claiming deterministic economic laws
- claiming policy causality from descriptive recurrence outputs
- claiming exact crisis timing forecasts from return-time summaries
- treating synthetic benchmark systems as empirical evidence

## Figure-level check

For every figure used in the article:

1. Confirm the figure is generated by code.
2. Confirm a source CSV exists.
3. Confirm the title does not imply causality or certainty.
4. Confirm the caption states what the figure shows and what it does not show.

## Table-level check

For every table used in the article:

1. Confirm the table is generated from repository outputs.
2. Confirm the underlying data is empirical if the prose is empirical.
3. Confirm any regime labels are descriptive rather than causal unless a separate causal design exists.

## Final wording check

Prefer:

- "is consistent with"
- "suggests"
- "describes"
- "indicates clustering"
- "highlights recurrence patterns"

Avoid:

- "proves"
- "shows causality"
- "demonstrates deterministic behavior"
- "predicts the next crisis date"
